Safety Standards Against Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE)

App: VibeNear - Spaces  |  Company: Vethuk Technologies pvt ltd

1) Zero-Tolerance Statement

VibeNear - Spaces maintains a zero-tolerance policy for any content or behavior involving the sexual abuse or exploitation of children. We prohibit the creation, possession, distribution, solicitation, or viewing of child sexual abuse material (CSAM) and any activity that facilitates grooming or exploitation of minors.

2) Prohibited Content & Activities

  • Any depiction of child sexual abuse or exploitation (real, fictionalized, or AI-generated).
  • Grooming, sexualized comments toward minors, or attempts to contact minors for sexual purposes.
  • Links to, instructions for finding, or tools for sharing CSAM.
  • Sexualization of minors (including “youth sexual content” even if age is ambiguous).

3) Reporting & Enforcement

Users can report suspected CSAE content or behavior via:

  • Email: support@vibenear.com

We promptly review reports, remove violating content, and take enforcement actions including account suspension or termination. Where legally required or appropriate, we will report to relevant authorities and/or hotlines (e.g., NCMEC or local equivalents).

4) Moderation & Detection Measures

  • Proactive and reactive moderation workflows to review flagged content.
  • Automation and human review where applicable and lawful.
  • Age-appropriate protections and restrictions for user interactions where relevant.

5) Cooperation with Authorities

We cooperate with law enforcement and recognized child protection organizations in accordance with applicable law to combat CSAE and safeguard victims.

6) Data Handling & Victim Safety

We handle reports and related data securely, limiting access to authorized personnel and retaining information only as long as necessary for safety, legal, and operational requirements. We prioritize victim privacy and safety in all processes.

7) User Education

We encourage all users to learn more about online safety and to report suspected exploitation. Resources may include local child protection hotlines and internationally recognized organizations.
